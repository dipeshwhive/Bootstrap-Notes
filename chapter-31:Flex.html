Flex Wrap

By default, flex items won't wrap if the container is too small. Use the .flex-wrap class to allow items to wrap, .flex-wrap-reverse to reverse the wrapping, or .flex-nowrap (default) to prevent wrapping.

No Wrap (Default)

Items will shrink to fit in one line. Items won't move to the next line; they'll stay on one line even if the container is too small (you may need to scroll horizontally to see all items).

Responsive Flex Classes

All flex classes come with additional responsive classes, making it easy to set a specific flex class on specific screen sizes. The * symbol can be replaced with sm, md, lg, xl, or xxl.

Examples of Responsive Flex Classes:

  • .d-md-flex - Display flex on medium screens and larger
  • .flex-md-row - Flex row direction on medium screens and larger
  • .flex-lg-column - Flex column direction on large screens and larger
  • .justify-content-md-center - Center justify content on medium screens and larger
  • .align-items-lg-center - Center align items on large screens and larger
  • .flex-wrap-md - Wrap items on medium screens and larger
  • .order-md-1 - Change order on medium screens and larger
